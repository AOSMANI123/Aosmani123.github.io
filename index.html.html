How I work

The parts that don't fit on a resume.

I adapt to the person, not the template

Supporting a CEO, a CISO's executive staff, and now a new leadership team means learning three different working styles fast. I treat each principal's preferences as the spec — communication channel, level of detail, when to interrupt and when to hold.

Autonomy is the default

Executive offices don't have time to supervise their support. I make the reversible decisions myself, document them, and bring only the genuinely two-way doors to the principal.

I've done the follow

I moved from Microsoft's Office of the CEO to Amazon's CEO Communications team — walking into an unfamiliar culture, learning its tools and writing standards, and shipping a working system within my first month.

Improv trained me for this

I perform improv and stand-up. The transferable skill is real: listen fully, say yes-and to the situation in front of you, and stay calm when the plan changes mid-scene. Which, in executive support, is every day.
